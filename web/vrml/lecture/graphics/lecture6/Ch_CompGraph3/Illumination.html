ILLUMINATION AND SHADING - PRINCIPLES


  • Visual Realism


    • creation of realistic images through
    - hidden surface removal
    - perspective projection
    - effect of light on color (photorealism):
      light sources
      surface reflection & absorption, translucency & refraction


  • Underlying Principles


    • - rays from a point light source emanate in all directions
    - rays are reflected/absorbed by surfaces (multiple times)
    - only rays that finally enter the eye contribute to image
    - only rays that pass through the view plane determine pixel color in bitmap
    - ray from a surface point to view point represents the accumulation of all reflection/absorption effects






  • Simplified Models for VR


    • - 'local' illumination models
    - only rays that come directly from light source are used to determine the color of a surface point
    - no inter-object reflections
    - indirect illumination approximated by 'ambient light'
    - no shadows (rays from light source penetrate objects)




  • Terms Used


    • - illumination model, lighting model, shading model, reflection model
    - rendering, rasterization
