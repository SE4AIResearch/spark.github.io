House on the Hills

Made using OPEN GL Programming with GLut32 &Glaux Library

'House on the Hills' is an effort to create a simulation of a neighborhood in a mountainous, snowy terrain using detailed polygons and objects. A unique 3-D camera perspective assists in letting the user explore the environment.

Sky view with mip mapped Skybox with texture mapped Mountains and Roads, Lamp Posts, trees,Car, ttrash Can, Letter Box

It consists of a 360-degree mip-mapped skybox that encloses a curved, snowy textured surface, which in turn holds a flat textured road that is blocked off on both ends.

Animation of car on the backdrop of snowy mountain

Around this road is a detailed neighborhood complete with a park and a fully-detailed interior of a house with forests around the back. Power lines, telephone poles, novelty trees, mailboxes, trash cans, and snow and gravel line the street.There are three prominent animations within the project, including a swingset with 2 swinging swings, a rotating merry-go-round, and a real, moving vehicle.

View of animated swings, swing set and merry go round

The lighting is made to be moody and atmospheric to portray the snowy, cold, and cloudy climate, and there is a slight silhouette of the hilly terrain if viewed at proper angles.

View showing snowy mountain neighborhood on the backdrop of snowy mountain

The larger house near the starting point is of particular interest, as it contains a detailed interior environment complete with a living room, kitchen, and bedroom. The living room features a carpet, a rug, a table on the rug, two couches, and paintings on the wall. Likewise, the kitchen and bedroom are full of detailed and textured furniture.

Interior of the house with Drawing Roon, Kitchen and Bed Room (click on image to enlarge)

The remaining houses feature fully detailed exteriors, including windows, doors, steps, sloped roofs.

View along the road

The vehicle is detailed with curved surfaces using hundreds of polygons and independently rotating tires, and is animated along track upon which it runs back and forth--first spinning its tires out, accelarating forward, burning out backwards and braking to a halt, and accelarating backwards. There is a proper attention to detail, as when the car is accelarating in reverse, the reverse tail lights toggle. A second program included on this CD details the car in particular.
