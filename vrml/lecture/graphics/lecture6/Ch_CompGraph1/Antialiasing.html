ANTIALIASING


  • Aliasing


    • Scan conversion (rasterization) of output primitives creates jagged displays, a problem called aliasing.


  • Antialiasing


    • - smoothing of jaggedness by altering pixel color or pixel gray level
    - requires multiple bits per pixel (multiple bitplanes)
    - area averaging technique:
      assume a line has a given width
      pixel intensity is determined by amount of area covered


Antialiased image (left) and pixel patterns in enlarged details (center and right)


  • Alternative Solution: Higher Resolution


    • The visual effect of aliasing will be reduced when using output devices with a higher absolute resolution (pixels per inch). The number of jags increases, but each jag is smaller.


Rasterized line on a low resolution device (left)
and on a device with twice the horizontal and vertical resolution (right)
