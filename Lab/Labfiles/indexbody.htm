Mission
 

      
The sensor network and Robotics laboratory consists of a number of temperature, humidity, motion, video sensors communicating on wireless network. Video sensor networks consists of stationary and mobile cameras with limited computational power and storage, but with a wireless interface to communicate and collaborate with each other in the network. The lab has a broad range of video camera sensors, communication devices and software development capabilities. Its data processing center is the Xseed, which is among TOP 200 fastest supercomputers in the world.

 

Activities
 

      
In addition, the lab allows robotics experimentation and research in mobile, autonomous sensors and in mobile base station (MBS) technologies to facilitate communication between and among peer sensors and control stations where the sensor data is stored in a database and used for system decision making. Research and Development of tools and methods for image processing, multi-agent systems and object detection.

 

Partners
 

      
Many of the current and past projects have been supported by industry and government agencies. We strongly believe in testing our research with practice and actively seek collaboration with industry.
